Botanical Information:
Scientific Name: Gossypium
Family: Malvaceae
Genus: Gossypium

Growth and Appearance
Cotton plants are shrubs, typically growing to a height of 3 to 6 feet. They have broad, lobed leaves and produce large, showy flowers that develop into cotton bolls containing cotton fibers.

Flowering and Fruiting
Cotton plants produce flowers with five petals, usually in cream or yellow colors. The fruit, known as a cotton boll, develops from the fertilized flowers and contains cotton fibers surrounding seeds.

Cultivation
Cotton is cultivated globally in warm climates. It requires well-drained soil, ample sunlight, and a frost-free period for optimal growth. Cotton is a major cash crop used for fiber production.

Varieties
There are several species of cotton, with Gossypium hirsutum (Upland cotton) being the most widely cultivated. Other species include Gossypium barbadense (Pima cotton) and Gossypium herbaceum (Levantine cotton). Each has unique fiber characteristics.

Uses
Cotton fibers are a valuable commodity used in the textile industry to produce a wide range of products, including clothing, bed linens, and industrial textiles. Cottonseed oil, extracted from cottonseeds, is also used in cooking and various industrial applications.

Economic Importance
Cotton is a significant economic crop, contributing to the textile industry and various other sectors. It is a vital cash crop for many countries and plays a crucial role in global economies.
