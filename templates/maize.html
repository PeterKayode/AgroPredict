Botanical Information:
Scientific Name: Zea mays
Family: Poaceae
Genus: Zea

Growth and Appearance
Maize, commonly known as corn, is an annual grass with a robust, tall stalk that can reach heights of 7 to 10 feet or more. It has large, broad leaves and produces ears containing rows of kernels.

Flowering and Fruiting
Maize plants produce separate male and female flowers on the same plant. The tassel is the male flower, located at the top of the plant, and the silk is the female flower, forming the corn ear.

Cultivation
Maize is cultivated globally and is a staple food in many cultures. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

Varieties
There are numerous varieties of maize, each adapted to different climates and purposes. Varieties may include dent corn, sweet corn, popcorn, and flour corn, each with distinct characteristics.

Culinary Uses
Maize is a versatile ingredient used in various culinary applications. It can be consumed as a whole ear, processed into cornmeal, corn syrup, or used in dishes like tortillas, polenta, and cornbread.

Economic Importance
Maize is economically significant worldwide, contributing to the food industry, animal feed production, and various industrial uses. It plays a crucial role in global agriculture and food security.
