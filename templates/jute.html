Botanical Information:
Scientific Name: Corchorus
Family: Malvaceae
Genus: Corchorus

Growth and Appearance
Jute plants are annuals with a height ranging from 6 to 12 feet. They have slender, cylindrical stems and large, coarse-textured leaves. Jute fibers are obtained from the outer bast layer of the plant.

Flowering and Fruiting
Jute plants produce small yellow flowers with five petals. The fruit is a capsule containing seeds. However, the primary economic value lies in the long, soft fibers found in the stem's bast layer.

Cultivation
Jute is cultivated in tropical regions with well-drained soil. It requires warm temperatures and high humidity for optimal growth. The plant is usually ready for harvest within 120 to 150 days after sowing.

Types of Jute
There are two main types of jute: White Jute (Corchorus capsularis) and Tossa Jute (Corchorus olitorius). Each type has specific fiber characteristics and uses.

Uses
Jute fibers are primarily used to make burlap, hessian, and gunny cloth. These materials are commonly employed for packaging, textiles, and various industrial applications. Jute is an eco-friendly and biodegradable fiber.

Commercial Importance
Jute has economic importance, particularly in the textile industry. It is a versatile and sustainable fiber, contributing to various products and industries globally.
