Botanical Information:
Scientific Name: Phaseolus vulgaris
Family: Fabaceae
Genus: Phaseolus

Growth and Appearance
Black bean plants are annuals, reaching a height of about 2 feet. They have compound leaves with three leaflets and produce pods containing black beans.

Flowering and Fruiting
Black bean plants produce small, white to violet flowers that develop into pods containing mature black beans. The beans are typically harvested when the pods dry.

Cultivation
Black beans are cultivated worldwide and thrive in warm climates. They are planted as seeds, and proper care includes well-drained soil and sufficient sunlight.

Varieties
There are various varieties of black beans, including Black Turtle beans and others, each with its unique characteristics.

Culinary Uses
Black beans are a versatile ingredient used in a variety of culinary dishes. They are commonly used in soups, stews, salads, and as a side dish.

Health Benefits
Black beans are a good source of protein, fiber, vitamins, and minerals. They are associated with heart health, weight management, and providing essential nutrients.
