Growth and Appearance
Pigeon pea plants are perennial legumes with a woody stem, typically growing to a height of 3 to 10 feet. They produce compound leaves with three leaflets and bear clusters of flowers that develop into long pods containing round seeds, which are the pigeon peas.

Flowering and Fruiting
Pigeon pea plants produce flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The pods develop from the fertilized flowers and contain edible seeds. Pigeon peas are typically harvested when the pods mature and the seeds reach their full size.

Cultivation
Pigeon peas are cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions with well-drained soil. They are known for their adaptability to various climates. Pigeon peas are grown for both their protein-rich seeds and as a cover crop to improve soil fertility.

Culinary Uses
Pigeon peas are a staple in many cuisines, especially in South Asia, Africa, and the Caribbean. They are used in a variety of dishes, including stews, curries, and rice-based meals. Pigeon peas are valued for their protein content and nutty flavor.

Nutritional Benefits
Pigeon peas are a good source of protein, fiber, vitamins, and minerals. They are considered a nutritious addition to a balanced diet and are often used in vegetarian and vegan recipes.
