Botanical Information:
Scientific Name: Lens culinaris
Family: Fabaceae
Genus: Lens

Growth and Appearance
Lentil plants are annuals with a bushy growth habit, reaching a height of about 18 inches to 2 feet. They have compound leaves with several pairs of leaflets and produce small pods containing lens-shaped seeds, which are the lentils.

Flowering and Fruiting
Lentil plants produce small, typically pale blue to white flowers that develop into pods containing lentils. Lentils vary in color and size, and their appearance depends on the specific variety.

Cultivation
Lentils are cultivated globally and are a vital food crop. They thrive in cool-season climates and well-drained soil. Lentils are often grown for their edible seeds and nitrogen-fixing properties.

Varieties
There are various varieties of lentils, each with unique characteristics. Common types include green lentils, red lentils, and brown lentils. The choice of lentil variety can affect cooking times and flavor.

Culinary Uses
Lentils are a versatile ingredient used in a variety of culinary dishes. They are commonly used in soups, stews, salads, and vegetarian dishes. Lentils are a rich source of protein, fiber, and essential nutrients.

Health Benefits
Lentils are nutritious and provide protein, fiber, vitamins, and minerals. They are associated with various health benefits, including heart health, digestive health, and weight management.
