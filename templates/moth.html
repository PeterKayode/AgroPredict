Growth and Appearance
Moth beans, also known as matki or dew beans, are annual legumes with a bushy growth habit. They typically reach a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plants produce trifoliate leaves and small, cylindrical pods containing small, oval-shaped beans.

Flowering and Fruiting
Moth bean plants produce small, yellow flowers that develop into slender pods. The beans are small, brown, and speckled, with a distinctive appearance. The beans are the edible seeds of the plant.

Cultivation
Moth beans are cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions. They thrive in well-drained soil and are known for their drought resistance. Moth beans are grown for both their edible beans and as a cover crop to enrich the soil.

Culinary Uses
Moth beans are a staple in various cuisines, especially in Indian and Southeast Asian dishes. They are used in curries, stews, salads, and sprouted for use in sandwiches and snacks. Moth beans are valued for their protein and nutritional content.

Nutritional Benefits
Moth beans are a good source of protein, fiber, vitamins, and minerals. They are considered a nutritious addition to a balanced diet and are often used in vegetarian and vegan recipes.

Traditional and Cultural Significance
Moth beans hold cultural and traditional importance in certain regions, where they are a dietary staple and play a role in local cuisines and rituals.
