Botanical Information:
Scientific Name: Mangifera indica
Family: Anacardiaceae
Genus: Mangifera

Growth and Appearance
The mango tree is an evergreen, reaching heights of 30 to 100 feet. It has a dense, rounded canopy of green, glossy leaves. Mango trees produce large, oval-shaped fruits with a single flat seed inside, known as a mango.

Flowering and Fruiting
Mango trees produce small, fragrant flowers in panicles, usually ranging from pale pink to white. The fruit develops from the fertilized flowers, with mango varieties varying in size, shape, and color.

Cultivation
Mangoes are cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions. They require well-drained soil, ample sunlight, and warm temperatures.

Varieties
There are numerous varieties of mangoes, each with unique flavors, textures, and colors. Common varieties include Alphonso, Haden, Kent, and Tommy Atkins, each prized for its taste and characteristics.

Culinary Uses
Mangoes are consumed fresh and are a popular ingredient in various culinary dishes. They are used in salads, smoothies, chutneys, desserts, and as a standalone fruit.

Commercial Importance
Mangoes have significant economic importance in international trade and local markets. They are a valuable export commodity, contributing to the agricultural economy of many countries.
