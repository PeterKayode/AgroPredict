Botanical Information:
Scientific Name: Phaseolus vulgaris
Family: Fabaceae
Genus: Phaseolus

Growth and Appearance
Kidney bean plants are annuals with a bushy growth habit, typically reaching a height of about 18 to 24 inches. They have compound leaves with three leaflets and produce kidney-shaped pods that contain beans.

Flowering and Fruiting
Kidney bean plants produce small, white to violet flowers that develop into pods containing mature kidney beans. The beans are typically harvested when the pods dry.

Cultivation
Kidney beans are cultivated globally and are an essential part of many cuisines. They require well-drained soil and sufficient sunlight. Kidney beans are grown for both their nutritious beans and as a nitrogen-fixing crop.

Varieties
There are various varieties of kidney beans, each with its unique characteristics. Common types are red kidney beans, white kidney beans, and speckled kidney beans.

Culinary Uses
Kidney beans are a staple in many dishes worldwide. They are used in soups, stews, salads, and as a side dish. Kidney beans are a good source of protein, fiber, and essential nutrients.

Health Benefits
Kidney beans are nutritious and provide protein, fiber, vitamins, and minerals. They are associated with various health benefits, including heart health, weight management, and blood sugar control.
