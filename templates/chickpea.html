Botanical Information:
Scientific Name: Cicer arietinum
Family: Fabaceae
Genus: Cicer

Growth and Appearance
Chickpea plants are annuals, growing to a height of about 18 inches to 24 inches. They have feathery pinnate leaves and produce small pods containing chickpeas.

Flowering and Fruiting
Chickpea plants produce white to violet flowers, and the pods contain mature chickpeas. The beans are typically harvested when the pods dry.

Cultivation
Chickpeas are cultivated globally and thrive in regions with cool temperatures. They are planted as seeds and require well-drained soil and moderate sunlight.

Varieties
There are various varieties of chickpeas, including Kabuli and Desi types, each with distinct characteristics.

Culinary Uses
Chickpeas are a versatile ingredient used in a variety of culinary dishes. They are commonly used in salads, stews, curries, and as the main ingredient in dishes like hummus.

Health Benefits
Chickpeas are a good source of protein, fiber, vitamins, and minerals. They are associated with digestive health, heart health, and providing essential nutrients.
